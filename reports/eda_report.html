Executive Snapshot

The project analyzes 7,728,394 US traffic accidents spanning January 14, 2016 through March 31, 2023. After cleaning, the repository works from 33 analytical columns, engineers 32 modeling features, and evaluates four binary classifiers where the target is Severity >= 3.

  • Severity 2 dominates the dataset at 79.67%, while the modeling target (Severity 3-4) accounts for 19.46%.
  • Accidents cluster around commute windows, with the highest hourly volumes at 7:00 and 16:00.
  • Friday has the highest accident count, and 69.33% of incidents occur during daylight.
  • California, Florida, and Texas lead total volume, with XGBoost delivering the strongest held-out performance (test AUC 0.9258).

Data Caveats

These are the most important constraints to keep in mind when interpreting this project’s results.

  • Visibility is constant at 10.0 miles after cleaning, so it contributes little usable variation to downstream analysis.
  • All road-infrastructure flags were removed before feature engineering, leaving `road_feature_count` constant at 0 for every row.
  • The 2023 slice only covers data through March 31, 2023, so year-over-year totals for 2023 are not directly comparable.
